Top Destinations in Uttarakhand

Discover the most breathtaking places in the heart of the Himalayas

Mussoorie - The Queen of Hills

Mussoorie, often called the "Queen of Hills," is one of India's most popular hill stations. Perched at an altitude of 6,000 feet, it offers breathtaking views of the snow-capped Himalayas and the Doon Valley.

Key Attractions:

  • Kempty Falls - A stunning waterfall perfect for picnics
  • Gun Hill - Second highest point offering panoramic views
  • Lal Tibba - Highest point in Mussoorie
  • Camel's Back Road - Scenic walking trail
  • Mall Road - Shopping and dining hub

Rishikesh - Yoga Capital of the World

Rishikesh, nestled in the foothills of the Himalayas on the banks of the Ganges, is renowned as the "Yoga Capital of the World." It's a spiritual haven and adventure hub combined.

Key Attractions:

  • Laxman Jhula & Ram Jhula - Iconic suspension bridges
  • Ganga Aarti - Evening prayer ceremony on the Ganges
  • River Rafting - Thrilling white water adventures
  • Yoga Ashrams - Spiritual learning centers
  • Neelkanth Mahadev Temple - Ancient Shiva temple

Nainital - Lake District of India

Nainital, surrounded by mountains and centered around the beautiful Naini Lake, is often called the "Lake District of India." It's a perfect blend of natural beauty and colonial charm.

Key Attractions:

  • Naini Lake - Boating and scenic views
  • Naina Devi Temple - Sacred hilltop temple
  • Tiffin Top - Panoramic viewpoint
  • Snow View Point - Cable car ride with mountain views
  • Eco Cave Gardens - Natural limestone caves

Valley of Flowers - Nature's Paradise

A UNESCO World Heritage Site, the Valley of Flowers is a high-altitude Himalayan valley known for its meadows of endemic alpine flowers and outstanding natural beauty.

Key Attractions:

  • Alpine Flowers - Over 500 species of wildflowers
  • Hemkund Sahib - Sacred Sikh pilgrimage site
  • Trekking Trails - Scenic mountain paths
  • Wildlife - Rare and endangered species
  • Photography - Stunning natural landscapes
