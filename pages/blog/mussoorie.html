Mussoorie - The Queen of Hills

Travel Guide • Uttarakhand • Hill Stations

Mussoorie, often called the "Queen of Hills," is one of India's most popular hill stations. Perched at an altitude of 6,000 feet, it offers breathtaking views of the snow-capped Himalayas and the Doon Valley. This charming hill station has been a favorite retreat for travelers since the British colonial era.

About Mussoorie

Located in the Dehradun district of Uttarakhand, Mussoorie stretches along a 15-kilometer ridge at an elevation ranging from 1,800 to 2,000 meters. The town gets its name from the Mansur shrub, which is native to the area. The cool climate, scenic beauty, and proximity to Delhi make it an ideal weekend getaway.

Key Attractions

  • Kempty Falls - A stunning waterfall perfect for picnics and photography. The falls cascade from a height of 40 feet, creating a beautiful natural pool at the bottom.
  • Gun Hill - The second highest point in Mussoorie, offering panoramic views of the Himalayas. Accessible by cable car, it's a must-visit for sunrise and sunset views.
  • Lal Tibba - The highest point in Mussoorie at 2,290 meters, providing spectacular views of the snow-clad peaks including Bandarpunch, Srikantha, and Pithwara.
  • Camel's Back Road - A scenic walking trail named after a rock formation that resembles a camel's hump. Perfect for morning walks and evening strolls.
  • Mall Road - The heart of Mussoorie, lined with shops, restaurants, and cafes. A great place to shop for local handicrafts and enjoy local cuisine.

Best Time to Visit

The best time to visit Mussoorie is from March to June (spring and early summer) and from September to November (autumn). During these months, the weather is pleasant with clear skies, making it perfect for sightseeing and outdoor activities.

How to Reach

Mussoorie is well-connected by road. The nearest airport is Jolly Grant Airport in Dehradun (60 km away), and the nearest railway station is also in Dehradun (34 km away). Regular buses and taxis are available from Dehradun to Mussoorie.

Travel Tips

  • Carry warm clothing even in summer as evenings can be chilly
  • Book accommodations in advance during peak season (May-June)
  • Try local delicacies like momos, thukpa, and Garhwali cuisine
  • Respect the environment and avoid littering
  • Wear comfortable shoes for walking and trekking
