Nainital - Lake District of India

Travel Guide • Uttarakhand • Hill Stations

Nainital, surrounded by mountains and centered around the beautiful Naini Lake, is often called the "Lake District of India." It's a perfect blend of natural beauty and colonial charm, making it one of the most popular hill stations in Uttarakhand.

About Nainital

Located at an altitude of 2,084 meters above sea level, Nainital is built around the emerald-shaped Naini Lake. The town is named after the Naina Devi Temple, which is located on the northern shore of the lake. The British discovered this beautiful location in 1841 and developed it as a summer retreat.

Key Attractions

  • Naini Lake - The heart of Nainital, this beautiful lake offers boating opportunities and scenic views. The lake is surrounded by hills on all sides, creating a picturesque setting.
  • Naina Devi Temple - A sacred hilltop temple dedicated to Goddess Naina Devi. The temple offers panoramic views of the lake and surrounding mountains.
  • Tiffin Top - Also known as Dorothy's Seat, this viewpoint offers breathtaking panoramic views of the Himalayas and the town below. It's accessible by a short trek or cable car.
  • Snow View Point - Reachable by cable car, this point offers stunning views of the snow-clad peaks including Nanda Devi, Trishul, and Nanda Kot.
  • Eco Cave Gardens - A network of natural limestone caves connected by narrow passages. It's a fun adventure for families and children.

Things to Do

Nainital offers a variety of activities for visitors. You can enjoy boating on the Naini Lake, take a cable car ride to Snow View Point, explore the local markets on Mall Road, visit the various viewpoints, or simply relax and enjoy the cool mountain air.

Best Time to Visit

The best time to visit Nainital is from March to June (spring and summer) and from September to November (autumn). During these months, the weather is pleasant with clear skies. The monsoon season (July-August) should be avoided due to heavy rainfall and landslides.

How to Reach

Nainital is well-connected by road. The nearest airport is Pantnagar Airport (68 km away), and the nearest railway station is Kathgodam (34 km away). Regular buses and taxis are available from major cities in Uttarakhand and nearby states.

Travel Tips

  • Book boat rides early in the morning to avoid crowds
  • Carry warm clothing as temperatures can drop significantly in the evenings
  • Try local street food but be cautious about hygiene
  • Respect the environment and avoid throwing waste in the lake
  • Wear comfortable shoes for walking and exploring the town
