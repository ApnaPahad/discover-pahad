Trekking & Hiking in Uttarakhand

Adventure • Uttarakhand • Trekking

Explore the pristine Himalayan trails with trekking adventures suitable for all levels. From easy day hikes to challenging multi-day treks, Uttarakhand offers some of the most beautiful and rewarding trekking experiences in India.

Popular Treks

  • Valley of Flowers Trek - A UNESCO World Heritage Site, this trek takes you through meadows of alpine flowers. The 17 km trek from Govindghat is moderate in difficulty and offers stunning natural beauty.
  • Kedarnath Trek - A spiritual journey to one of the Char Dham pilgrimage sites. The 16 km trek from Gaurikund to Kedarnath is challenging but deeply rewarding, passing through beautiful mountain landscapes.
  • Har Ki Dun - Known as the Valley of Gods, this trek takes you through remote villages and alpine meadows. The moderate trek offers a glimpse into traditional Garhwali culture.
  • Roopkund - The Mystery Lake Trek is one of the most famous treks in Uttarakhand. The challenging trek leads to a high-altitude lake surrounded by human skeletons, creating an air of mystery.

Trekking Difficulty Levels

Treks in Uttarakhand are classified into different difficulty levels:

  • Easy - Suitable for beginners, mostly flat or gentle slopes, 1-3 days
  • Moderate - Requires basic fitness, some steep sections, 3-5 days
  • Difficult - Requires good fitness and experience, high altitude, 5-7 days
  • Very Difficult - For experienced trekkers only, extreme altitude, 7+ days

Best Time for Trekking

The best time for trekking in Uttarakhand is from April to June (spring and early summer) and from September to November (autumn). During these months, the weather is pleasant, skies are clear, and trails are accessible. The monsoon season (July-August) should be avoided due to heavy rainfall and landslides.

Essential Gear

  • Sturdy trekking boots with good ankle support
  • Backpack (40-60 liters for multi-day treks)
  • Warm clothing and rain gear
  • Sleeping bag and tent (for camping treks)
  • First aid kit and personal medications
  • Water bottles and water purification tablets
  • Headlamp or flashlight with extra batteries
  • Trekking poles for support

Safety Tips

  • Always trek with a guide or experienced group
  • Acclimatize properly before high-altitude treks
  • Carry sufficient water and stay hydrated
  • Inform someone about your trekking plans
  • Respect the environment - leave no trace
  • Be prepared for sudden weather changes
  • Carry a map and compass or GPS device
