River Rafting in Uttarakhand

Adventure • Uttarakhand • Water Sports

Experience the thrill of white water rafting in the Ganges River. Rishikesh offers some of the best rafting experiences in India with rapids ranging from Grade I to Grade V. Whether you're a beginner or an experienced rafter, there's an adventure waiting for you.

About River Rafting

River rafting in Uttarakhand, particularly in Rishikesh, has become one of the most popular adventure activities in India. The Ganges River provides perfect conditions for rafting with its combination of calm stretches and challenging rapids, all set against the backdrop of the majestic Himalayas.

Best Locations

  • Rishikesh - The most popular rafting destination in India. The 16 km stretch from Shivpuri to Rishikesh offers rapids ranging from Grade I to Grade IV, suitable for both beginners and experienced rafters.
  • Alaknanda River - Known for its challenging rapids, the Alaknanda offers more technical rafting experiences. The river is perfect for those seeking an adrenaline rush.
  • Bhagirathi River - Offers scenic routes with a mix of calm waters and exciting rapids. The river flows through beautiful valleys and provides stunning views of the surrounding mountains.

Rafting Grades

Rafting routes are classified by difficulty grades:

  • Grade I-II - Easy, suitable for beginners and families
  • Grade III - Moderate difficulty with some challenging rapids
  • Grade IV - Difficult, requires experience and physical fitness
  • Grade V - Very difficult, only for expert rafters

What to Expect

A typical rafting trip includes safety briefing, equipment distribution, and a trained guide. The journey takes you through beautiful gorges, past ancient temples, and through thrilling rapids. Most trips include breaks for swimming and cliff jumping.

Best Time to Go

The best time for river rafting in Uttarakhand is from September to June. The monsoon season (July-August) should be avoided due to high water levels and increased risk. The water is coldest in winter but the rapids are most challenging during the pre-monsoon period (May-June).

Safety Tips

  • Always wear a life jacket and helmet provided by the operator
  • Listen carefully to the safety briefing and follow your guide's instructions
  • Choose a registered and licensed rafting operator
  • Inform your guide about any medical conditions
  • Carry a waterproof bag for your belongings
  • Wear appropriate clothing - quick-dry clothes and water shoes
