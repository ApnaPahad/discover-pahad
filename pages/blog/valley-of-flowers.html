Valley of Flowers - Nature's Paradise

Travel Guide • Uttarakhand • UNESCO World Heritage Site

A UNESCO World Heritage Site, the Valley of Flowers is a high-altitude Himalayan valley known for its meadows of endemic alpine flowers and outstanding natural beauty. Located in the Chamoli district, this valley is a paradise for nature lovers, trekkers, and photographers.

About the Valley

The Valley of Flowers National Park spans over 87 square kilometers and is situated at an altitude ranging from 3,200 to 6,675 meters above sea level. The valley was discovered by British mountaineer Frank S. Smythe in 1931 and was declared a national park in 1982. It was inscribed as a UNESCO World Heritage Site in 2005.

Key Attractions

  • Alpine Flowers - The valley is home to over 500 species of wildflowers, including rare and endangered species. The best time to see the flowers in full bloom is from July to September.
  • Hemkund Sahib - A sacred Sikh pilgrimage site located at an altitude of 4,329 meters. The gurudwara is surrounded by seven snow-clad peaks and a crystal-clear lake.
  • Trekking Trails - The valley offers some of the most scenic trekking routes in the Himalayas. The trek from Govindghat to the valley is approximately 17 km and takes 2-3 days.
  • Wildlife - The park is home to rare and endangered species including the Asiatic black bear, snow leopard, blue sheep, and various species of birds.
  • Photography - The valley provides stunning natural landscapes perfect for photography, especially during the monsoon season when flowers are in full bloom.

The Trek

The journey to the Valley of Flowers begins from Govindghat, which is accessible by road from Rishikesh or Haridwar. From Govindghat, you trek to Ghangaria (14 km), which serves as the base camp. From Ghangaria, it's a 3 km trek to the valley entrance. The valley itself requires another 3-4 km of walking to explore fully.

Best Time to Visit

The Valley of Flowers is open from June to October, but the best time to visit is from mid-July to mid-August when the flowers are in full bloom. The valley remains covered in snow during the winter months and is inaccessible.

Important Information

  • Entry permits are required and can be obtained at the Forest Department office in Ghangaria
  • The valley is open from 7 AM to 5 PM
  • Camping is not allowed inside the valley
  • Visitors must return to Ghangaria by evening
  • Carry sufficient water and snacks as there are no facilities inside the valley

Travel Tips

  • Start the trek early in the morning to avoid afternoon rains
  • Carry rain gear as the weather can change quickly
  • Wear comfortable trekking shoes with good grip
  • Acclimatize properly before starting the trek
  • Respect the environment - do not pluck flowers or disturb wildlife
  • Carry a camera to capture the stunning beauty
